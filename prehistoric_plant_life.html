Prehistoric Plant Life

The plant life of the Mesozoic Era created lush, verdant landscapes that served as home and sustenance for the dinosaurs that roamed the Earth.

Ferns and Cycads

Ferns were abundant during the age of dinosaurs, creating dense understories in prehistoric forests. Cycads — palm-like plants with thick trunks and crowns of large compound leaves — were a major food source for many herbivorous dinosaurs.

Conifers and Ginkgos

Coniferous trees dominated many prehistoric landscapes, forming vast forests that provided shelter and food. Ginkgo trees, which still exist today, were also widespread and offered nutritious leaves for browsing dinosaurs.

The Rise of Flowering Plants

During the Cretaceous period, flowering plants (angiosperms) began to diversify rapidly, transforming ecosystems and creating new ecological niches. This “Cretaceous Explosion” of plant diversity changed the face of Earth forever.

Ancient Ecosystems

These plants formed complex ecosystems that supported not only dinosaurs but also insects, early mammals, and other creatures. The interaction between plants and animals drove evolutionary changes that shaped life as we know it today.
